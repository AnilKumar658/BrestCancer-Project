Brain Tumor Detection Project

Project Overview

This project focuses on detecting brain tumors from MRI images using advanced deep learning techniques and image processing methods. Our goal is to provide a reliable and efficient system for early detection of brain tumors, which is crucial for timely medical intervention.

Methodology

The project employs Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) to classify MRI images into different categories: glioma, meningioma, notumor, and pituitary. The methodology includes data preprocessing, model training, and performance evaluation. We utilize a dataset of labeled MRI images for training and validation.

Results

The results of our project demonstrate high accuracy in classifying MRI images. The trained model shows promising performance metrics, including precision, recall, and F1-score. Detailed analysis of the results and comparisons with existing methods are provided in the full report.
